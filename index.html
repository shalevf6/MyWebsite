  2. "Explain the differences between Web 1.0, Web 2.0 and Web 3.0 through the use case - "Purhcasing a watch".

    3. Web 1.0

    In order to purchase a new watch, you would have to enter every website (that you know of) that has watches for sale and search it for the car that you want. You could also find a shopping portal that would have addresses of sites that sell watches in it.

    Web 2.0

    You could search sites like ebay for watches, Meaning, you could buy a watch from private people, and not only from stores, whether it's a used watch or a new one. With that, you could have a larger comparison of prices and variety to conclude which watch you would like to purchase.

    Web 3.0

    You can go to a site like BestBuy and enter all your preferences for your desired watch. The site will then use its algorithms to list the top matches for your preferences, and will recommend you other products which you would likely want to purchase as well.
